Giraffes Can Dance

Adapted from “Giraffes Can’t Dance” by Giles Andreae

Lucy was a tall giraffe
Whose neck was long and slim,
But her knees were awfully crooked
And her legs were rather thin.

She was very good at standing still
And munching shoots off trees,
But when she tried to run around
She buckled at the knees.

Now every year in Africa
They hold a Jungle Dance,
Where every single animal
Turns up to skip and prance.

This year when the day arrived
Lucy put on her dancing shoes.
Even though she had never danced before
She knew there was nothing to lose.

The warthogs started waltzing
And the rhinos rock ‘n’ rolled.
The lions danced a tango
Which was elegant and bold.

The chimps all did a cha-cha
With a very Latin feel,
And eight baboons then teamed up
For a splendid Scottish reel.

Lucy looked focused
As she approached the dance floor.
She listened to her heartbeat
Even as the lions roared.

Hey, look at clumsy Lucy!
The animals all sneered,
But Lucy paid no attention
And smiled from ear to ear.

The laughter continued
From everyone around.
They thought they saw a circus,
But it was they that were the clowns.

When Lucy finished dancing
She simply turned and headed home.
Her confidence was brimming
She could move a mountain all alone.

She stopped at a little clearing
and she looked over at the bend.
The world can be so beautiful.
I want this day to never end.

Excuse me! Coughed a cricket
who’d seen all that Lucy had.
How is it that you are so happy?
On the dance floor, you were kinda bad.

Lucy was not offended
She knew the cricket was not wrong.
Good and bad are relative
If we are singing the same song.

And so that is why I am happy
I choose to dance just for me
I determine what is good and bad
And pay no attention to what others see

With that, the cricket smiled
and picked up his flute.
Truthfully, I could never play it
I just thought that it looked cute

So he started blowing
He hit a note here and there.
He understood what Lucy meant
And made music without a care.

Then each animal from the dance
approached with a look of dread.
Their eyes were on the ground
They must have heard what Lucy said.

The lion whispered, I love math
But I’m afraid how it makes me look,
And a baboon confessed,
My dream is to write a book.

But that was not all
The best was saved for last.
Two chimps screamed I LOVE YOU!
And embraced without being asked.

Lucy raised her head
and looked up at the moon and stars above.
What a mess we’ve created,
But at least we’ll do what we love.
